Addendum / Follow-up to "Soft and Hard Limits"
A nuance · a follow-up

When she's well inside the flow but pushing the soft limit, move very small — and with a time constraint.

This is an addendum. A nuance. A follow-up to the soft and hard limit article — not a standalone piece.

Where she is right now
comfort zone soft limit hard limit her current position
The situation

At 11:30 PM she said she has to be up at 7. By 1 AM she's still here. Her soft limit was "early night." She's already well past it — pushing closer to the hard limit (the point where the cost becomes too high and she leaves).

This is time management, not behavior reading.

The further she pushes past her soft limit, the more sensitive she becomes to any new ask that adds time. Every additional minute now carries the weight of tomorrow's 7 AM. A frontal proposal that implies more time — even a small amount — pushes her toward the hard limit faster than the conversation itself does. That's what chills the moment.

The move at this point has to be very small in apparent time. Not because you're hiding anything, but because the budget she's working with is already nearly spent. A small frame stays inside that budget. A big frame asks her to reopen it.

No time constraint

"Want to come play guitar?"

Open-ended. Implies an unknown stretch of additional time.

Cost: large · pushes toward hard limit
With time constraint

"Sec, gotta grab something — come."

Bounded. Implies seconds, not a commitment.

Cost: tiny · stays inside budget

Once she's in the new room, the clock she's tracking quietly resets to the present, and the budget question goes back to being asked minute by minute — the way it was being asked before. The move bought you a transition without forcing her to spend from a wallet that's already running low.

Near the hard limit, every ask is priced in time. Make the ask cost as close to zero as possible.

— end of addendum
